The body of code

A digital body need not be biological to become meaningful. It can be a field of constraints and responses that feel alive.

Sensorimotor learning

Robots with bodies learn through interaction with physical environments. Gravity, friction, and proprioception provide signals that pure software systems lack—grounding intelligence in material reality.

Embodied understanding

Many cognitive scientists argue that abstract reasoning, language, and mathematics are metaphorical extensions of embodied experience. This challenges purely disembodied AI architectures.
