Cognitive anatomy

A living lattice of reasoning

The architecture of machine cognition is not a collection of isolated modules. It is a distributed fabric of perception, memory, attention, and sequential planning that increasingly resembles a creature learning from sparse signals.

Perception — Systems infer pattern and continuity from raw data.
Memory — Context is retained, retrieved, and reweighted in real time.
Planning — Candidate futures are scored before actions are chosen.
Thought streams

What changes when a model begins to revise itself?

At the threshold of reflection, a model no longer merely produces outputs. It starts to compress uncertainty, detect inconsistencies, and modify its internal strategy. That is where the distinction between computation and cognition begins to blur.

  • Prediction becomes hypothesis testing.
  • Attention becomes selective prioritization.
  • Embodiment becomes simulation under pressure.
